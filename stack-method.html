THE METHOD

The STACK Method.

Five letters, five places a hire can break. A shared language for diagnosing hiring problems before throwing money at them.

Fractional recruiting fits small businesses with ten to two hundred employees making a few hires per year, in industries where referrals have stopped scaling and contingency fees have started stacking. It does not fit enterprises with internal talent teams, venture-funded startups looking for a single senior hire, or businesses that hire one person every three years. We work with one client per competitive lane.
The STACK Method is Metcalf Search's five-letter diagnostic for small business hiring: Source, Tempo, Assess, Close, Keep. Every hiring problem lives in one of the five. Name the letter and you know what to fix.

Most hiring advice starts with tactics. STACK starts with location. Before you rewrite the job ad, raise the salary, or add another interview, find out where the search is actually breaking. The five letters below each open into a full field guide. Tap a letter, see the questions that live there, and read the leaf that matches yours.

Every hiring problem you face lives in one of five places. Find yours.

SOURCEAttract

When the right candidates can't find your job, or won't apply when they do.

  • Why aren't qualified candidates applying to my job?
  • How do I write a job title that candidates actually search for?
  • How does a small business compete with bigger companies for talent?
  • Where should I be posting jobs that aren't Indeed?
Read the SOURCE leaf →
TEMPOEngage

When candidates ghost, your process stalls, and the good ones accept somewhere else first.

  • Why do candidates ghost us before the interview?
  • How fast do I need to respond to applicants?
  • What does a good first message to a candidate look like?
  • We're losing candidates between interview and offer. Why?
Read the TEMPO leaf →
ASSESSVet

When the resumes look right, the interviews go well, and you still can't tell who will do the job.

  • Why do I keep making bad hires even when interviews go well?
  • What's the difference between a structured interview and a regular one?
  • Should I be using a scorecard for hiring?
  • How do I assess for skills I don't have myself?
Read the ASSESS leaf →
CLOSEOffer

When offers get rejected, counter-offers win, and your top choice walks at the eleventh hour.

  • How do I make an offer a candidate won't refuse?
  • What do I do when a candidate gets a counter-offer?
  • How much salary negotiation room should I leave?
  • Why do candidates accept and then back out?
Read the CLOSE leaf →
KEEPRetain

When new hires quit in 90 days, in a year, in two, and you start the whole search over again.

  • Why do new hires quit in the first 90 days?
  • What does a good onboarding process actually include?
  • How do I retain employees as a small business?
  • Should I be doing stay interviews?
Read the KEEP leaf →

How the method is used

Inside a Fractional Recruiting partnership, STACK is how the pod organizes the work: every search gets a sourcing plan, a tempo commitment, a structured assessment, an offer sequence, and a ninety-day keep plan. On your own, it works as a diagnostic. When a search stalls, find the letter where it stalled and start there. Tiers and pricing are on Services.
